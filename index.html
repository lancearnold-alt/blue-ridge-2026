One Drive, Two Reasons

One long drive up into the Blue Ridge — and two completely separate reasons to be there.

The first is a wedding. Kaylee & Ben are getting married in Tuckasegee, North Carolina on Saturday, September 5th — the anchor of the whole trip and its own event entirely.

The second is long overdue: once the celebrating is done, we're staying in the mountains for two unhurried days with Sam & Jen, who are driving over from the Lexington, Kentucky area. Not a quick dinner — real time together, in one of the prettiest corners of the state.

To be clear, these are two unrelated things that happen to share the same week and the same mountains. Different people, different plans — the wedding crowd and Sam & Jen don't know each other, and that's exactly as it should be. This page just follows the trip from one to the next.

Part TwoTwo Days in the Mountains with Sam & Jen

The main narrative — a slow, scenic reunion once the wedding's behind us

Sam & Jen drive in from the Lexington area (~5 hrs) and meet us at a cabin on Bear Lake in Tuckasegee — a quiet mountain lake that makes an ideal home base, about 15 minutes from Cashiers. Two full days, deliberately unhurried.
Sunday — Waterfalls, Cashiers & SylvaSeptember 6
Morning

Sam & Jen arrive at the Bear Lake cabin. Coffee on the porch, no rush — ease into the day and let everyone shake off the drive.

Midday — an easy waterfall or two
  • Silver Run Falls — a 25-ft falls and swimming hole just off Hwy 107 south of Cashiers, a quarter-mile stroll from the road
  • High Falls at Lake Glenville — a 100+ ft falls on the West Fork of the Tuckasegee, a short hike with a big payoff
Afternoon — Sylva

Drive over to Sylva and wander Main Street below the photogenic Jackson County Courthouse. Coffee at City Lights Cafe (attached to the bookstore) or Blue Ridge Bootleg Coffee, then a pint at Innovation Brewing or Lazy Hiker.

Evening

Dinner back toward Cashiers — The Ugly Dog Public House or Whiteside Brewing Co. — then back to the cabin for the good part: the porch, a fire, and a long catch-up with nowhere to be.

🏡 Cabin on Bear Lake, Tuckasegee — home base both nights
Monday — The Appalachian Trail DaySeptember 7 · Labor Day
🥾 The one big hike — planned for today, rested, after the wedding weekend
Morning — Siler Bald on the A.T.

Drive to Wayah Gap near Franklin (~1 hr) and hike the Appalachian Trail to Siler Bald — about 4 miles round trip, moderate, climbing to a wide grassy summit with 360° views across the Nantahala National Forest. A genuine A.T. day hike without needing any backpacking gear, and the kind of view you plan a trip around.

Rested, on purpose: saving the real hike for after the wedding means nobody's cramming it in tired. Weather in the mountains turns fast in September, so we'll check the forecast that morning and keep it flexible.
Afternoon — the Blue Ridge Parkway

On the way back, hop on the Blue Ridge Parkway for the drive to Waterrock Knob (MP 451), the highest visitor center on the Parkway, with a short walk to a near-360° overlook of the Great Balsams and Smokies. Pull off at Richland Balsam, the Parkway's highest point, along the way.

Evening

Last dinner together — maybe back in Sylva or Cashiers — then a final night on the cabin porch. Slow evening, no schedule.

Tuesday — Drive HomeSeptember 8
🚗 Tuckasegee → Tampa · ~9–9.5 hrs · Sam & Jen ~5 hrs to Lexington

One last cabin breakfast, then everyone points home — us south to Tampa, Sam & Jen back toward Lexington. Long drive, full week, worth every mile.

Places We'll Explore

Real spots around Tuckasegee, Cashiers, Highlands & Sylva — enjoy the region, no checklist required

Waterfall · Easy

Silver Run Falls

A 25-ft falls into a calm pool just off Hwy 107 south of Cashiers — a quarter-mile round trip, the definition of a low-effort, high-reward stop.

Waterfall

High Falls, Lake Glenville

A 100+ ft cascade on the West Fork of the Tuckasegee near Cashiers — on dam-release days it becomes one of the most powerful falls in the state.

Waterfall · Scenic Drive

Dry Falls & Bridal Veil Falls

Two roadside falls on US-64 toward Highlands — you can walk behind Dry Falls (75 ft), and Bridal Veil drips right beside the old highway.

Waterfall · Tallest in NC

Whitewater Falls

At 411 ft, the tallest falls in the East. A short paved walkway leads to the upper overlook — an easy add-on if the group wants one more.

Overlook

Whiteside Mountain

A 2-mile loop on some of the oldest cliffs on Earth, between Cashiers and Highlands, with big drop-off views over the valleys.

Mountain Town

Downtown Sylva

Walkable Main Street with the most-photographed courthouse in NC, three coffee shops and three breweries — the region's easy-hangout town.

The Hike

🥾 Siler Bald via the Appalachian Trail

From Wayah Gap near Franklin, ~4 miles round trip to a grassy 5,200-ft bald with 360° views across the Nantahalas. Moderate, memorable, and the planned centerpiece of the getaway — saved for the rested day after the wedding. Nearby Wayah Bald adds a short paved walk to a historic stone lookout tower if the group wants more.

Scenic Drive

🚗 Blue Ridge Parkway to Waterrock Knob

The stretch near Balsam climbs past Richland Balsam (the Parkway's highest point, 6,053 ft) to Waterrock Knob (MP 451), where a short walk opens a near-360° view over the Great Balsams, Smokies, and Nantahalas. Best light is late afternoon — a natural pairing with the hike day.

Home Base

🏡 A Cabin on Bear Lake, Tuckasegee

A pristine 476-acre mountain lake at 2,500 ft, ~15 minutes from Cashiers and right where the wedding is. This is where the getaway really lives — porch coffee, a grilled dinner, a fire, board games, and long conversations. The lake and the towns are close enough that we never have to backtrack or over-drive.

Two reasons, one beautiful week

A wedding we wouldn't miss, and two days with old friends we've put off for too long — stitched into one drive up into the Blue Ridge. Different people, different plans, the same mountains. Mostly, it's about being there for the people who matter and coming home with a full tank of good memories.
